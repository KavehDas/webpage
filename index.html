About Me

My name is Kaveh Dastouri, and I was born in Heris, East Azerbaijan, Iran, in 1995. I completed my bachelor’s degree at Farhangian University, followed by a master’s degree in algebra at the University of Tabriz. I earned my Ph.D. in mathematics at Tarbiat Modares University in Tehran under the supervision of Prof. Ali Iranmanesh. I recently completed a postdoctoral position at Tarbiat Modares University, where my research focused on representation theory and finite groups. In December 2025, I began a new postdoctoral appointment at the Beijing International Center for Mathematical Research (BICMR).

Research Interests

  • Representation Theory of Finite Groups: Including ordinary and modular cases.
  • Finite Groups and Their Applications: Especially in combinatorics.
  • Public Key Cryptosystem Scheme Design: Using hard computational representation problems.

Current Projects

  • Researching π-partial character degrees and their influence on the structure of groups.
  • Engaging with classical number theory problems, applying representation theory approaches.
  • Creating public key cryptosystems.
  • Creating a package for π-partial characters and π-structure of groups in GAP.
